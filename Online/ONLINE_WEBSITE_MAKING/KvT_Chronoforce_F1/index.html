Stempline Garage

The Stempline Garage is a 360° hub for experimentation, STEM research, and hands-on development in the world of F1 in Schools. Purpose-built to accelerate student innovation, it's where engineering meets creativity, and where raw ideas are transformed into high-performance design. Methodology
.01
Concept
and Design
Concept
and Design
We initiate the process by brainstorming and sketching initial car concepts, focusing on aerodynamics and compliance with F1 in Schools regulations. Utilizing Autodesk Fusion 360, we create detailed 3D models, allowing for precise control over dimensions and design features. This stage ensures our designs are both innovative and manufacturable.
.02
Simulation
and Optimization
Simulation
and Optimization
Before physical production, we employ Autodesk Flow Design to simulate aerodynamic performance, identifying areas for improvement. This virtual wind tunnel analysis enables us to refine our designs, optimizing for speed and stability on the track.
.03
Result Manufacturing
and Testing
Result Manufacturing
and Testing
Post-optimization, we generate CAM toolpaths within Fusion 360 to guide CNC machining of the car components. After manufacturing, we conduct rigorous testing to validate performance, making necessary adjustments to achieve optimal results.
.01
Sportware
Sportware
A fusion of performance, innovation, and purpose. Sportware is our custom-engineered solution built for the F1 in Schools challenge — lightweight, aerodynamic, and designed for peak racing efficiency. Every detail is crafted to give our team the competitive edge, where speed isn’t just a goal — it’s the standard.

Car Technology
Car Technology
At KvT Chronoforce, our car isn’t just a model — it’s a micro-engineered masterpiece. Designed using industry-grade tools like Autodesk Fusion 360 and SolidWorks, every curve, cut, and component is optimized for aerodynamic precision. From low-drag profiles to CO₂-powered acceleration systems, our technology reflects real-world F1 innovation scaled into a classroom revolution. This is the future of STEM in motion.

Designing and engineering cars

From initial concept to wind tunnel simulations, it's a full-stack engineering journey where design meets performance, and precision fuels innovation.
